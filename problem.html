Part One: The Problem

Rubbish, it's everywhere

In an era of mass production and globalisation, the by-product cannot be escaped. Let's face it, trash it everywhere these days, but Barry Curtis Park has been hit especially hard. Once a serene park with vast fields and glistening waterways, the trash throwing bullies of society has beaten out the beauty of old Barry Curtis Park. This isn't just a local problem, though, it's everywhere in Auckland, New Zealand, Oceania and worldwide.

What's causing it?

You may be asking, what is causing it? The lack of bins? The lack of punishments for littering? The social norms that govern us behaviour? Well, it's a mix of all of all of them. Studies have found that people tend to litter in areas where there is already a prominent level of trash. This feedback loop is just going to accelerate the normalisation of littering.

Who's responsible?

You may also be asking, who's responsible for all this trash? In Barry Curtis Park, there are potentially thousands of visitors per year, with many having picnics and social gatherings where sole use items are everywhere. Some may be lazy, some may be evil, but most are just ignorant to the consequences of their actions.

About Barry Curtis Park

Barry Curtis Park is a park named after Barry Curtis in Flat Bush. At 94 hectares (230 acres), it is one of New Zealand's largest parks.
