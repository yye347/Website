There is a problem...

At Barry Curtis Park, there has been a rubbish problem that has been haunting visitors and locals. Plastic bags, food wrappers, plastic bottles, food waste and even shopping trolleys have invaded the natural habitat of hundreds of native faunae. The consequences of this trash have massive implications for the future Barry Curtis Park, and the cleanliness of the local environment as a whole. Four Ormiston Senior College students took notice of this problem and as a result, the group have formed a team to spread awareness and clean up the waste polluting this beautiful park. As nearby residents of the park, we have a mission to serve our local community for a more enjoyable and natural Barry Curtis Park.

It's getting worse.

If we don't act now, it will only get worse over time. Everyday there is more rubbish appearing in creeks and along walkways. The animals need a break from all the rubbish, and Barry Curtis Park needs a thorough detox of all the pollution that has invaded its land.

The solution is in your hands...

We cannot do it ourselves; we need YOU! Not only you but your family and friends, neighbours, and co-workers. Together, we can achieve something no individual can themselves. Please take responsibility for your rubbish, while cleaning up the land that you live on. Think about the long-term consequences, the implications this has on your kids, your grandkids, and their kids. Don't let our generation become selfish, and take initiative to look after mother nature. Please gift your time for the benefit of everyone by clicking the button below.

Together we can help stop it.
